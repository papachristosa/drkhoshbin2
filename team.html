About Dr. Khoshbin

Orthopaedic surgeon, researcher, and Division Chief of Orthopaedic Surgery at St. Michael's Hospital.

Dr. Amir Khoshbin is an orthopaedic surgeon with subspecialty expertise in adult hip and knee reconstruction and trauma. His elective practice centres on direct anterior hip replacement, complex and revision hip reconstruction, and the management of complex lower-limb trauma, including pelvic and acetabular fractures.

He completed his undergraduate and medical degrees at the University of Toronto, where he also trained in orthopaedic surgery. During residency he joined the Surgeon Scientist Program and earned a Master of Science in Clinical Epidemiology. He then completed fellowship training in orthopaedic trauma at Massachusetts General Hospital and Brigham and Women's Hospital in Boston, through the Orthopaedic Trauma Association, followed by a second fellowship in Adult Reconstruction and Joint Replacement at the Hospital for Special Surgery in New York.

Dr. Khoshbin is Division Chief of Orthopaedic Surgery at St. Michael's Hospital and holds the inaugural Agnico Eagle Chair in Joint Arthroplasty. He is the 2025 Chair of Research for the Canadian Arthroplasty Society and Chair of Research for the Division of Orthopaedic Surgery at the University of Toronto.

He has authored more than one hundred peer-reviewed publications and has received research funding from organisations including the OTA, AAHKS, COA, AAOS, CIHR, and AO Trauma. He is senior investigator on three international randomized controlled trials.

The team

Who you will meet

Dr. Khoshbin works with a close team. Referrals come first to the hip and knee Rapid Access Clinic (RAC), where advanced practice clinicians triage them, see patients in consultation, and follow them after surgery. It is how patients are seen quickly and followed consistently, with Dr. Khoshbin involved at every step that needs him.

Angelo Papachristos

BSc, BScPT, MBA, ACPAC
Advanced Practice Physiotherapist · Rapid Access Clinic

An ACPAC-trained Advanced Practice Physiotherapist at St. Michael's Hospital, where he has worked since 1998, and clinic lead for the hospital's hip and knee Rapid Access Clinic. In the RAC he triages referrals, assesses patients in consultation, and follows them through recovery after joint replacement, with a wider caseload spanning arthroplasty, trauma, sports medicine, and rheumatology. He helped build the hospital's rapid-access model and its work on patient-reported outcomes and bundled funding, and has co-authored studies on surgical and non-surgical care for knee osteoarthritis and a quality improvement bundle that reduced opioid overprescribing after hip and knee replacement. A member of the first graduating class of the Advanced Clinician Practitioner in Arthritis Care (ACPAC) program through U of T and St. Michael's (2006), he holds Adjunct Lecturer status in the Department of Physical Therapy at the University of Toronto, lectures across North America, and co-founded RheumAcademy and Arthros, Canadian education and virtual-care platforms for rheumatology. B.Sc.(PT), U of T, 1998; MBA, 2007.

Caroline Jones

BScPT, MRSc, ACPAC
Advanced Practice Physiotherapist · Rapid Access Clinic

An Advanced Practice Physiotherapist in the hip and knee Rapid Access Clinic at St. Michael's Hospital, where she has spent her entire career: a physiotherapist from 1987 and in the advanced-practice role since 2006. Alongside Dr. Khoshbin she triages referrals, assesses patients in consultation, and follows their recovery after surgery. She has co-authored work on multidisciplinary models of care for osteoarthritis, comparative outcomes of surgical and non-surgical treatment for knee osteoarthritis, and a quality improvement bundle that reduced opioid overprescribing after hip and knee replacement. She completed the Advanced Clinician Practitioner in Arthritis Care (ACPAC) Program through U of T and St. Michael's in its first graduating class (2006), and holds a Master of Rehabilitation Science from UBC (2010) and a B.Sc.(PT) from U of T (1987). She held Lecturer status in the Department of Physical Therapy at U of T from 2013 to 2016, and her work has been recognised with St. Michael's Leadership and Quality Improvement Award and the Health Disciplines Interprofessional Collaboration Award.
